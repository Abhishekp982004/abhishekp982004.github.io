    Radiation pollution and its impact on living creatures in and around Bangalore

    From smartphones in our pockets to complex communication systems, electronic devices are ubiquitous, emitting radiation that permeates our environment. This study delves into the potential consequences of this growing presence, focusing on both human health and the natural world. While widespread smartphone use appears harmless to humans due to their average radiation levels, the same can't be said for small birds like sparrows, whose populations face endangerment due to this increasing radiation pollution. Furthermore, this non-ionizing radiation disrupts signal communication, causing challenges like coverage and reception issues. To address these concerns, radio systems are increasingly incorporating spectrum analysis tools, though their limitations necessitate more advanced solutions. Enter Real-Time Spectrum Analysers (RTSAs), capable of measuring wideband spectrums like those emitted by electronic devices. This study utilized the Tektronix RSA306, a portable and powerful tool for "radiation hunting" across various regions. Its ability to measure radiation within a range of 9 kHz to 6.2 GHz proved invaluable. Data gathered from four core locations in Bangalore was analysed to assess radiation pollution levels. The findings revealed average radiation strength of 2.42 GHz across all observations, falling below the World Health Organization's (WHO) threshold limit for frequencies ranging from 900 MHz to 1800 MHz This suggests no current negative health impacts on humans in Bangalore due to signal radiation. Looking ahead, the study employed linear regression and graphical representation to predict potential radiation increases within the next three years. These insights can serve as valuable tools for future monitoring and potential mitigation strategies. By examining the impact of electronic devices on both humans and the environment, this study highlights the need for continued research and awareness to ensure responsible technology use and a sustainable future.     Modern Fire Alarm System

    Fire hazards pose a serious threat to life and property, making early detection and rapid response essential. This project presents a smart Fire Alarm System that integrates multiple sensors to detect potential fire risks in real time. The system utilizes a Gas/Smoke Sensor (MQ-2), Temperature Sensor (LM35CAZ) and Flame Sensor to monitor environmental conditions and identify fire- related anomalies. When a fire hazard is detected, the system triggers an alarm(buzzer and LED light indication) and sends instant alerts to the system with a UI website which will display all the real time values that the system measures and cautions the user in case of a Fire. Additionally real-time Temperature value(in °C) and flame/gas indication(Boolean) will be displayed on an inbuilt LCD display. It can be run on a 9V battery(can also be powered by the system when connected).

    We have also incorporated detailed analysis using multiple graphs(temperature and gas values plotted against time-utilizes latest 100 data points) based on various data points collected during fires which helps in prediction and analysis of potential fire risks which can be avoided in the future. We have developed an eco-friendly, lightweight, highly durable and cost-efficient model with a minimalistic design. This model is tested multiple times under all possible circumstances with an excellent accuracy and precision.​

    This model is highly scalable and IoT-enabled solution for fire detection, improving response times and minimizing potential damage in residential and commercial spaces. This model breaks the barriers and drawbacks of traditional Fire Alarm Systems through modern technological integrations. This model can also be used to alert remote users through a Wi-Fi module(ESP8266/ESP32) or a GSM module making it highly flexible. You can find the overview.
